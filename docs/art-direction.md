# Art Direction Prompt — Optiml 3.0 Website
## Direction 1: Pure Minimal SaaS

Design a B2B enterprise SaaS website for **Optiml**, a Real Estate Decision Intelligence platform. The site must feel governance-grade, category-defining, and institutionally credible — not a startup, not an ESG tool, not a dashboard. Think: Ramp meets a Swiss architecture firm.

---

## Colour System

- Primary: Electric blue `#2B4EFF` (used for CTAs, accent blocks, illustration strokes, key labels)
- Background: Near-white `#F7F8FA` — light, airy, clinical
- Dark contrast sections: Deep navy `#0A0F2E` (for CTA banners, footer, proof sections)
- Neutral: `#E4E7F0` for card borders, dividers, subtle UI elements
- Text: `#0A0F2E` on light, `#FFFFFF` on dark

---

## Typography

- Display / Headings: A confident, tight grotesque — Neue Haas Grotesk, ABC Favorit, or similar. Large, bold, wide tracking. No decorative serifs.
- Body: Clean, readable — `DM Sans` or `Sora`. Small size, generous line height.
- Labels / metadata: Uppercase, tracked, small — used for section tags, proof labels, stat descriptors

---

## Hero Section

- Full-bleed or split-panel layout
- Left: Large editorial headline (2–3 lines max), one-line subheading, two CTA buttons (primary: blue filled, secondary: ghost)
- Right: Premium enterprise stock photography — a boardroom, IC meeting, or architectural overhead shot. No lifestyle. No smiling people pointing at screens.
- Thin horizontal rule below nav. Minimal nav: logo left, links centre, CTA button right.

---

## Illustration Style (Body Sections)

- Blueprint-style technical vector line art — single blue stroke on white/off-white
- Isometric or flat exploded diagrams (reference: "Making Software" by Dan Hollick spread)
- Used for: feature icons, section graphics, how-it-works diagrams, artifact previews
- Stroke weight consistent, no fills except for accent blue on key components
- Grid lines, dashed connectors, annotation-style labels permitted

---

## Cards & Infographic Sections

- White cards, 1px `#E4E7F0` border, 12–16px radius, subtle drop shadow
- Stat blocks: Large numerals in blue, small uppercase descriptor below (Ramp-style)
- Proof bars / trust signals near every CTA
- Decision Record previews shown as structured document UI cards — labelled fields, audit trail rows, status chips

---

## Layout Principles

- 12-column grid, generous gutters
- Sections breathe — padding-heavy, never cramped
- Occasional full-bleed blue band for CTA or category statement
- Asymmetric content pairings: large left headline + tight right body copy
- Section labels in small uppercase blue above every heading block

---

## What to Avoid

- No purple gradients, glassmorphism, or generic AI-SaaS blobs
- No dark mode hero — light-first throughout
- No "Book a demo" as the only CTA
- No ESG/sustainability visual language (no green, no leaf icons, no carbon imagery)
- No dashboard-heavy UI screenshots as the primary hero visual

---

## Tone

Restrained. Confident. Institutional. The visual equivalent of a Goldman Sachs report designed by a Swiss modernist studio.
