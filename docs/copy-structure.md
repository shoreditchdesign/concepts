## Section 5 — How REDI Works

**Preheading:** How it works

**H2 (47 chars):** From inputs to a Decision Record in three steps

**Step 1 — Data in**
Start with an address and basic asset data. Optiml's enrichment layer fills gaps using localised sources — energy benchmarks, regulatory thresholds, cost indices. Low data quality is fine to begin.

**Step 2 — Optimisation runs**
The engine works across the KPIs your IC tracks: ROI, Capex, NOI, CO₂ reduction, EPC ratings, CRREM compliance. The same inputs produce the same outputs, every time. That's not a feature — it's the point.

**Step 3 — Decision Record out**
A structured output with visible assumptions, traceable logic, and a refresh path when constraints change. Download as PDF or Excel. Present it directly.

**Note:** AI handles data enrichment. The optimisation is deterministic — algorithmic, not generative.

## Section 10 — FAQ

**Preheading:** Questions we hear before sign-off

**H2 (46 chars):** Questions fund managers ask before signing off

**Q: How is this different from the BI or ESG tools we already use?**
Those tools produce data. Optiml produces a Decision Record — a structured output with a traceable logic chain, built to hold up in IC and lender review. It's not a reporting layer. It replaces the process of producing committee-ready decisions.

**Q: Can the Decision Record be audited independently?**
Yes. Every output includes input assumptions, optimisation logic, and KPI outputs in a format auditors and lenders can follow. Nothing is opaque in the engine.

**Q: What data do we need to get started?**
An address, asset class, and basic building data. Optiml's enrichment layer fills the rest. You can begin at low data quality and build up as the process deepens.

**Q: What happens when constraints change — rates, regulations, Capex budgets?**
You refresh the record. The engine reruns on updated inputs and produces a new output. You don't rebuild from scratch.
